Constant Contact vs HubSpot: The Full Head-to-Head Comparison

Let us walk through every dimension that matters to a small business owner. We tested both platforms hands-on, reviewed verified user feedback from G2, Capterra, and Trustpilot, and looked at pricing data as of July 2026.

Pricing

Constant Contact's Lite plan starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. The Standard plan, which includes automation and A/B testing, starts at $35 per month. The Premium plan with full segmentation and reporting starts at $80 per month. All prices are for monthly billing; annual billing saves you roughly 15%.

HubSpot offers a free plan, but it is heavily limited. You can send 2,000 emails per month and your emails will include a HubSpot-branded footer that you cannot remove. HubSpot's Marketing Hub Starter begins at $20 per month for 1,000 contacts. Marketing Hub Professional — where you unlock automation workflows, A/B testing, and custom reporting — starts at $890 per month. That is not a typo. Most of HubSpot's genuinely useful marketing features require a Professional or Enterprise plan.

Ease of Use

Constant Contact is one of the most beginner-friendly email platforms ever built. The drag-and-drop editor is straightforward. You can upload your contact list in CSV format, pick a template, write your email, and hit send in under 30 minutes. The interface assumes you are not a developer. Customer support via phone, chat, and email is included on every paid plan.

HubSpot has improved its user experience significantly, but it is still a CRM platform that happens to include email marketing — not an email marketing platform that happens to have a CRM. There are contacts, companies, deals, tickets, properties, views, pipelines, and sequences all competing for your attention before you ever build your first email. If you have time to learn the system, HubSpot becomes very powerful. If you are a solo founder or a two-person team, the complexity is a real cost.

Email Templates and Design

Constant Contact offers over 200 mobile-responsive email templates organised by industry and occasion. The templates look professional without any design experience. You can also build your own from scratch with the drag-and-drop editor.

HubSpot's template library is smaller on lower-tier plans, and many of the most polished templates require custom coding or access to the Design Tools, which are best used by someone with HTML and CSS knowledge. On the free and Starter plans, your design options are limited.

Automation

Constant Contact's automation covers everything a small business actually needs: welcome email series for new subscribers, birthday and anniversary emails, automated resends to non-openers, and e-commerce triggers like abandoned cart reminders (on Standard and above). You set these up in a visual flow builder that takes about 20 minutes to learn.

HubSpot's workflow automation is among the most powerful in the industry, but you need a Professional plan to access it. On the free and Starter plans, you get only basic follow-up email sequences. If you are paying $890 per month, the automation is genuinely exceptional — branching logic, lead scoring, CRM property updates, internal notifications, and cross-channel triggers. For a 15-person sales and marketing team, that is worth the money. For a local restaurant or a three-person e-commerce shop, it is not.

List Management and Segmentation

Both platforms handle list management competently. Constant Contact lets you tag contacts, create segments based on engagement, location, and custom fields, and automatically suppress unsubscribes and bounces. The segmentation tools on the Premium plan are genuinely powerful for a tool at this price point.

HubSpot's contact management is where it genuinely shines. Because it is a CRM at heart, every contact record is rich with interaction history, sales pipeline data, website visits, and deal stages. If you are running a B2B company where knowing which contacts visited your pricing page three times matters for your sales team, HubSpot is a better fit. If you run a bakery or a yoga studio, you do not need any of that.

Deliverability

Both platforms consistently achieve inbox placement rates above 95% in independent testing by Email Tool Tester and SendForensics. Constant Contact enforces strict list hygiene policies at signup, which helps first-time senders avoid deliverability mistakes that tank their sender reputation. HubSpot's deliverability is equally strong but requires more technical setup — specifically configuring your DKIM, DMARC, and SPF records correctly, which can be a barrier for non-technical users.

Integrations

Constant Contact connects to over 300 apps including Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, Eventbrite, QuickBooks, Facebook, Google Ads, and WordPress. For most small businesses, the integration library covers everything needed.

HubSpot's integration ecosystem is larger — over 1,500 apps — and its native integrations with tools like Slack, Zoom, LinkedIn, and Salesforce are more deeply built. If your business runs on a complex tech stack and you need all your tools talking to each other, HubSpot wins on integrations.

The Situations Where HubSpot Actually Makes More Sense

HubSpot is not the wrong answer for every business. Here is when you should genuinely consider it.

If you have five or more salespeople managing deals in a pipeline, and you want your marketing emails to automatically update contact records, trigger sales tasks, and score leads by behaviour — HubSpot Professional is worth every penny. How to Get the Most Out of Constant Contact From Day One

If you have decided Constant Contact is the right tool — and for most small businesses reading this, it is — here are the six things to do in your first week to make sure you see real results fast.

1. Import Your Contacts in the Right Format

Export your existing customer list from your POS system, CRM, or spreadsheet as a CSV file. Include first name, last name, email address, and any relevant tags (for example: "in-store customer," "online buyer," "newsletter signup"). Constant Contact uses these tags immediately for segmentation.

2. Set Up a Welcome Email Before You Do Anything Else

Go to the Automation tab, click New Automation, and select "Welcome new subscribers." This single email — triggered the moment someone joins your list — typically generates 3x to 4x the open rate of a regular campaign. It takes 15 minutes to set up and runs forever without you touching it again.

3. Send Your First Campaign to Your Warmest Segment

Do not blast your entire list on day one. Start with your most engaged contacts — people who have bought from you in the last six months. Write a short, personal email. Include one clear offer. One link. One ask. Measure the response before you go broader.

4. Connect Your Shopify or WooCommerce Store

If you sell online, connecting your store takes about five minutes inside the Integrations tab. Constant Contact will automatically start syncing purchase history data to each contact record. This unlocks product recommendation emails and purchase-triggered automations that run passively and generate consistent revenue.

5. Enable Resend to Non-Openers

After you send any campaign, Constant Contact lets you automatically resend the same email with a different subject line to everyone who did not open it the first time. Turn this on for every campaign. Industry data shows this single step increases your total reach by 30% to 40% per send.

6. Check Your Reports Every Two Weeks

Constant Contact's reporting dashboard shows you open rate, click rate, bounce rate, and unsubscribes for every campaign. Focus on your click-to-open rate — that tells you whether your content is resonating after people open the email. Aim for a click-to-open rate above 10%. If you are below that, test a different call-to-action button or a more specific offer.

Frequently Asked Questions: Constant Contact vs HubSpot

Is Constant Contact cheaper than HubSpot?

Yes, significantly. Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. HubSpot's Marketing Hub Starter begins at $20 per month but its free plan removes most useful features. Once you need more than 1,000 contacts, HubSpot pricing jumps sharply. For most small businesses, Constant Contact is the more affordable long-term choice by a large margin.

Can HubSpot replace Constant Contact for email marketing?

Technically yes, but it is overkill for most small businesses. HubSpot is built around a full CRM platform. If you only need to send email campaigns, manage a list, and track open rates, Constant Contact does all of that with far less complexity and cost. HubSpot makes more sense if your team also runs paid ads, manages a sales pipeline, and builds landing pages simultaneously.

Which tool is easier to learn: Constant Contact or HubSpot?

Constant Contact is easier by a wide margin. You can build and send your first campaign in under 30 minutes. HubSpot has hundreds of settings, objects, and workflows that take days to understand fully. If you have no dedicated marketing staff, Constant Contact lets you get results immediately without a learning curve that costs you time and money you cannot afford to lose.

Does HubSpot have better automation than Constant Contact?

HubSpot's automation is more powerful, but you only access the full workflow builder on Professional plans starting at $890 per month. Constant Contact's automation covers the essentials: welcome series, birthday emails, resend to non-openers, and abandoned cart triggers. For under $50 per month, Constant Contact automation is more than sufficient for most small business needs.

Which platform has better deliverability?

Both platforms maintain strong deliverability with inbox placement rates above 95% in independent tests. Constant Contact has a slight edge for small senders because it enforces stricter list hygiene policies upfront, protecting your sender reputation from day one. HubSpot deliverability is excellent too, but requires more technical configuration around DKIM and DMARC records to achieve the same results.
