What Each Tool Actually Delivers (Plain Talk)

Mailchimp — Great to Start, Gets Expensive Fast

Mailchimp's free plan is genuinely useful if you have fewer than 500 contacts and can live without phone support. The drag-and-drop editor is clean, and the templates look modern. The problem arrives the moment your list grows. At 2,500 contacts, Mailchimp's Essentials plan costs $45 per month. Constant Contact handles that same list size for $35 per month and throws in phone support. Mailchimp also removed its free plan's automation features in 2023 — you now need a paid plan to run even a basic welcome email sequence.

Bottom line: Mailchimp wins on brand recognition and design flexibility, but it is not the budget option people assume it is once your list gets past the free tier. For a deeper comparison, see our Constant Contact vs. Mailchimp head-to-head.

Brevo — The Best Pick If You Need SMS Too

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) prices by emails sent rather than contacts stored. That means if you have a big list but only email them twice a month, Brevo can be very affordable. Its free plan lets you store unlimited contacts and send 300 emails per day. The paid Starter plan at $9 per month removes the daily sending cap. Brevo also handles SMS campaigns from the same dashboard — a genuine advantage if you run a restaurant, retail shop, or service business where text reminders drive foot traffic. The downside: Brevo's automation builder is less intuitive than Constant Contact's, and support can be slow on the lower tiers.

Klaviyo — Powerful for Online Stores, Overkill for Everyone Else

Klaviyo is built specifically for e-commerce. It syncs with Shopify, WooCommerce, and BigCommerce to track what your customers browse, add to cart, and buy. You can then trigger emails like abandoned cart reminders and post-purchase upsells automatically. That power comes with complexity — Klaviyo assumes you know what a "flow" is and why segment conditions matter. If you run an online store doing more than $5,000 per month in revenue, Klaviyo's analytics alone can justify the cost. If you run a local service business or a brick-and-mortar shop, it is almost certainly more tool than you need.

AWeber — Reliable Workhorse With a Loyal Following

AWeber has been around since 1998 and its reputation for reliability is well earned. Deliverability is solid, phone support is genuinely helpful, and the platform is stable. The free plan covers up to 500 subscribers. Where AWeber falls short is in design — its templates feel dated compared to Mailchimp or Constant Contact, and the interface has not had a major overhaul in years. For bloggers and newsletter creators who just want to send reliably without fuss, AWeber is a dependable choice. For small businesses that want polished branded emails, it feels behind the times.

How to Choose the Right Tool for Your Business (A 5-Minute Process)

Do not get paralyzed comparing features you will never use. Answer these four questions and you will have your answer in five minutes.

1. How big is your list right now?

If you have under 500 contacts and are testing the idea of email marketing for the first time, Mailchimp's free plan or Constant Contact's 60-day trial both make sense. If you have 500 to 5,000 contacts, Constant Contact's pricing is competitive with or cheaper than every other paid platform at that size.

2. Do you run an online store?

If yes and you are doing significant revenue through Shopify or WooCommerce, give Klaviyo serious consideration. Its abandoned cart and browse-abandonment flows can generate revenue automatically once set up. If you run a local or service business, skip Klaviyo — the complexity will not pay off for you.

3. Do you need SMS alongside email?

If text message marketing is part of your plan — appointment reminders, flash sale alerts, reservation confirmations — Brevo is the cleanest way to manage both channels in one place. Otherwise, the SMS feature is not worth switching platforms for.

4. How much time can you spend learning a new tool?

If the answer is "not much," Constant Contact is the right pick. Its onboarding is genuinely the fastest in the industry. You get guided setup, live support, and an editor that behaves like what you already expect. You will not be watching YouTube tutorials to figure out how to change a button color.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Mailchimp cheaper than Constant Contact in 2026?

Mailchimp has a free plan for up to 500 contacts, which is cheaper to start. But once your list grows past 500 contacts, Mailchimp's paid plans become more expensive than Constant Contact's entry price of $12 per month. For growing small businesses, Constant Contact usually wins on overall value — and includes phone support that Mailchimp reserves for higher-tier plans.

Which Constant Contact alternative is best for e-commerce?

Klaviyo is the strongest pick for e-commerce because it syncs deeply with Shopify and WooCommerce, tracking what customers browse and buy. That said, Constant Contact also has solid e-commerce integrations and is easier to set up for store owners who are not technical. If you run a small shop and want simplicity, Constant Contact still delivers strong results without the learning curve.

Can I switch from Constant Contact to another tool easily?

Yes. You can export your contact list as a CSV from Constant Contact and import it into any major platform within minutes. Most tools also offer free migration support. The harder part is recreating your automations and templates, so allow a few hours for that work before you cancel your existing account. Always send one final campaign before switching to keep your list warm.

What is the easiest email marketing tool for a complete beginner?

Constant Contact consistently ranks as the easiest platform for beginners. Its drag-and-drop editor, guided onboarding, and live phone support mean you can send a professional-looking campaign on your first day without watching a single tutorial. Brevo is a close second for simplicity, but Constant Contact's support quality — including real phone access on entry-level plans — is unmatched in the industry right now.

Does Constant Contact offer a free trial?

Yes. Constant Contact offers a 60-day free trial with no credit card required. That gives you two full months to build lists, send campaigns, and see real results before you spend a dollar. Very few competitors match that trial length — Mailchimp's free plan is ongoing but restricted, while most others cap trials at 14 or 30 days.
