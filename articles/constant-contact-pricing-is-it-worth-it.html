What You Are Actually Paying For

Constant Contact has three paid tiers: Lite, Standard, and Premium. Pricing is based on list size, not the number of emails you send — so you can email your whole list every single day without paying extra per send.

Here is what each plan covers at the 500-contact level:

  • Lite — $12/month: Unlimited email sends, 300+ templates, basic list management, event marketing tools, and real-time reporting. Phone and chat support included.
  • Standard — $35/month: Everything in Lite, plus automated email series, contact segmentation, A/B subject line testing, scheduled sends, and Google Ads integration.
  • Premium — $80/month: Everything in Standard, plus advanced segmentation, custom automation paths, SEO tools, and dedicated account support.

For most small businesses — a local salon, a boutique retailer, a freelance consultant, a nonprofit with a donor list — the Lite plan at $12 per month is where you start. And that entry price is the crux of the whole question: is $12 per month worth it?

Why the Pricing Question Is the Wrong Starting Point

Most people approach email marketing tools the same way they approach a Netflix subscription — they look at the monthly cost and ask whether they can afford it. That is the wrong frame entirely.

Email marketing is not an expense. It is a revenue channel. The question is not "can I afford $12 per month?" The question is "what does $12 per month generate for my business?"

According to research from Litmus, the average return on email marketing is $36 for every $1 spent. That is not an outlier or a best-case scenario — it is the industry average across small and medium businesses. For a business spending $12 per month on Constant Contact, the average expected return is $432 per month in revenue attributable to email campaigns.

Here is a practical example. You own a coffee shop with 400 email subscribers. You send one email promoting a new seasonal drink and a limited-time loyalty offer. Even if just 5% of your list clicks through and visits, that is 20 customers. If each spends $15, you have just generated $300 from a $12 investment. That math works everywhere — from home services to e-commerce to professional services.

This is why email marketing has the highest ROI of any digital marketing channel, beating paid social ads, SEO, and display advertising. Your email list is an audience you own outright. No algorithm can cut your reach. No platform can suspend your account and make your audience disappear overnight.

How to Get Maximum Value From Your Constant Contact Plan

Signing up is step one. Getting real ROI requires a few intentional moves. Here are practical steps to make Constant Contact worth every cent from day one.

Import your existing contacts immediately

Constant Contact lets you import contacts from a CSV file, Gmail, Outlook, Excel, or directly from your Shopify or e-commerce store. Do this before you do anything else. Your first campaign should go to real people who already know your business — not a cold list.

Use a welcome email as your first send

A welcome email — sent automatically when someone joins your list — gets open rates between 50% and 60% on average. That is 3 to 4 times the open rate of a standard promotional email. Set one up during your free trial. Make it personal: thank them for signing up, tell them what to expect, and include one clear offer. This one automation alone can justify the monthly cost.

Send at least two campaigns per month

One email per month is not a strategy — it is an accident. The businesses that see strong ROI from email marketing send consistently. Two to four emails per month is the sweet spot for most small businesses. Use Constant Contact's calendar view to plan one month ahead so you are not scrambling for content at the last minute.

Check your reports after every send

Constant Contact's reporting dashboard shows you open rate, click rate, bounce rate, and unsubscribes for every campaign. After your first few sends, you will see patterns — certain subject lines outperform others, certain send times get more opens. Use this data to improve every subsequent campaign.

Upgrade strategically, not automatically

The Standard plan at $35 per month adds automation sequences and segmentation. Do not upgrade until you have hit the ceiling of what the Lite plan offers. A good rule: once you have a list over 500 contacts and you are sending regularly, consider Standard for the automated drip sequences. For most businesses under 2,000 contacts, Lite is plenty.
